Startups

Companies I've helped build or have started, including projects that may not even be around anymore.

Arkmont

https://arkmont.com/

Arkmont.com is an online education marketplace that enables anybody with a skill the ability to teach online. We call these people teachers, and teachers are able to build their online reputation as ane xpert in their field while increasing their income.

We focus on high quality education for the masses and there's no insane discounting. The entire idea behind Arkmont is to enable teachers to do what they do best: share their knowledge and expertise — we'll handle the rest.

I am the founder and CEO of Arkmont.com. Currently interim CTO, too.

Xcellerator

https://xcelleratortools.com/

Xcellerator helps Amazon FBA sellers never run out of stock by adjusting the quantity of items they order from their manufacturers and when the orders should take place.

This service works with numerous API's, including Amazon Marketplace Web Services. The company is registered in the United Kingdom.

I was the sole developer (full-stack), Sys Admin and general technology manager. Xcellerator is still around and doing well.

Footrr

https://footrr.com/

Footrr allowed anyboy to add an img HTML tag to their email signatures and instantly start serving advertisements via email. The image dynamically changed every time it was loaded based on ranking algorithms. Users made 30% of the CPC bid and were paid via PayPal.

The service became very popular in India, and eventually Footrr was spending more time fighting fraud than it was growing the business. The cost of running Footrr with all the fraud became too high, and after 2 years it was time to shut down.

I was the founder and CEO.

Kalast

https://kalast.com/

Kalast is a small business marketplace with an exclusive social aspect, built just for small business owners. The all-in-one loyalty card allows users to throw out all their other loyalty cards and stamp cards, and use the custom branded Kalast card (or just their phone number instead). Customers can give businesses reviews, and businesses can leave notes on customers, track their Kalast-driven customers and put their business online with a strictly business-only profile.

I was the founder and CEO at Kalast, Inc. until August 2014 when it was acquired by Everyday Communities.
